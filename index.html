KHADI

KHADI ALSO REFERRED AS KHADDAR IS AN INDIAN HOMESPUN COTTON CLOTH . A COTTON CLOTH OF PLAIN WEAVE, PRODUCED IN INDIA . HOWEVER, THE DEFINITION WAS DIFFERENT IN PRE INDEPENDENCE ERA. KHADI IS AN ETHOS FORMULATED BY MAHATMA GANDHI DURING SWADESHI MOVEMENT BECAUSE THE COTTON CLOTH KHADI WAS EASILY ACCESSIBLE.

personal view

I belong to East Champaran district (Bihar) from where Mahatma Gandhi started 'SATYAGRAHA' movement and on that day he also insisted everyone to use local fabric 'KHADI' . In those days Champaran district was known for KHADI GRAM UDYOG and my grand parents told me that many people were dependent on that industrty for survival and the products were of the finest quality and were sold all over in India. But now only two manufacturing units are remaining and even those are in worst condition, so to make people aware of this industry and it's importance, I am putting a little bit of effort to create this digital research paper so that one can access the knowledge and information about 'KHADI'.

Old is gold

INDIA IS HOME TO THE WORLD’S MOST HAND-SKILLED ARTISANS AND HAS A RICH TEXTILE HERITAGE. A HERITAGE OF HIGH-END QUALITY CRAFTSMANSHIP AND DIVERSITY RANGING FROM HAND EMBROIDERY, BLOCK PRINTING, NATURAL DYEING, HAND WEAVING, TO THE MASTERY OF HAND SPINNING. IT IS IN THIS MAGICAL COUNTRY AND BY ITS SKILLED ARTISANS WHERE THE ROOTS OF “KHADI” FABRIC CAN BE FOUND. UNLIKE FROM OTHER FABRICS, KHADI HAS MARKED AS A TESTAMENT OF INDIA'S PAST AND IS PROOF THAT “OLD IS TRULY GOLD”. TRADITIONAL KHADI IS HAND-SPUN AND HAND-WOVEN COTTON FABRIC ALSO MANUFACTURED FROM WOOL AND SILK, KNOWN AS KHADI SILK OR WOOLEN KHADI. HAND-SPUN YARNS WITH COUNTS AS HIGH AS 200, ALMOST INVISIBLE TO THE HUMAN EYE, ARE HAND-TWISTED AND -WOVEN INTO BEAUTIFUL FABRICS. THESE TECHNIQUES CAN ONLY BE MASTERED BY FEW DUE TO ITS DIFFICULTY. THE QUALITY DIFFERENCE OF A THREAD THAT IS FORMED BETWEEN TWO FINGERTIPS EPITOMIZES THE FABRIC’S UNIQUE CHARACTER AND IDENTITY. THE FACTORS WHICH DISTINGUISH KHADI FROM HANDLOOM PRODUCTS IS THAT, KHADI IS HAND-SPUN WITH THE HELP OF A CHARKHA (SPINNING WHEEL) AND ON THE OTHER HAND HANDLOOM YARN IS PROCESSED IN THE MILL. THIS IS WHAT MAKES KHADI FABRIC SO UNIQUE AND RESILIENT AND INCORPORATES THE PROPERTIES OF KEEPING THE WEARER WARM IN WINTER AND COOL IN SUMMER. THE PRODUCTION OF KHADI IS AN EXTREMELY JUDICIOUS PROCESS WHICH FOCUSES ON THE ENVIRONMENT RIGHT FROM THE BEGINNING OF THE YARN SPINNING TO THE WHOLE PROCESS OF WEAVING. THIS IS WHAT MAKES KHADI FABRICS UNIQUE IN TERMS OF PRODUCTION, AND IMPOSSIBLE TO COMPARE OR COPY BY MACHINE-MADE FABRICS. BEFORE INDEPENDENCE KHADI WAS CONSIDERED AS THE FABRIC FOR THE POLITICAL LEADERS AND THE RURAL PEOPLE. BUT NOW IT HAS MADE A REMARKABLE MARK INTO THE WARDROBES OF FASHION CONSCIOUS PEOPLE. THE CURRENT SCENARIO IS THAT THE DEMAND OF KHADI IS MORE THAN ITS SUPPLY. EARLIER ONLY KHADI COTTON WAS MANUFACTURED WHICH HAVE A VERY COARSE TEXTURE AND FEEL. NOW, MANY VARIETIES OF KHADI LIKE KHADI SILK, KHADI WOOL AND KHADI POLY-VASTRA ARE BEING MANUFACTURED FROM WOOL, SILK AND BLENDED FIBERS, WHICH MAKE KHADI A FASHIONABLE FABRIC

Data research

Application of khadi

From the survey, we came to know that most of the people use khadi for home textile & furnishing and many people also use it for apparel and other use.

Pride of nation

From the survey, we came to know that most of the people wear khadi because it gives us a sense of nationalism .

Consideration of Khadi

From the survey, we came to know that most of the people use khadi as casual or occasional wear but many people don't agree that Khadi is fashionalable.

Identification of Khadi

From the survey, we came to know that most of the people identify Khadi by its texture while some people identify by it's appearance and colour.

Variety of Khadi

From the survey, we come to know that most of respondant prefer to use cotton fabric and some people want silk and poly Khadi
